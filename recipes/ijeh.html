Ijeh
source: Palestine on a Plate: Memories from My Mother's Kitchen
ingredients needed
ijeh salsa
❤ 1 small onion, chopped ❤ 2 lg tomatoes, finely chopped
❤ 1 green chili, chopped ❤ juice of 2 lemons
❤ 8 eggs, beaten ❤ 3/4 c olive oil
❤ 1-2 garlic cloves, finely chopped ❤ 1 garlic clove, grated
❤ 1 1/2 tbsp al purpose flour ❤ sea salt
❤ 1/4 teaspoon baking powder ❤ a sprinkle of dried mint
❤ 1 tbsp dried mint ❤ a small bunch of fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
❤ 1 tomato, deseeded and cubed
❤ a bunch of fresh flat-leaf parsely, chopped
❤ a few leavs of fresh mint, chopped
❤ sea salt and black pepper
❤ veggie oil for frying
steps
1. To make the salsa, mix all the ingredients together and set aside until needed
2. In a bowl, mix all the ijeh ingredients, except the oil, to make a batter
3. Heat a teaspoon of oil in a frying pan over high heat until it's very hot. Drop ladlefuls of the batter into the hot pan in batches (adding more oil for each fritter) and cook for about 2 minutes until they are lightly browned. Turn the fritters over to cook for about 2 minutes until they are lightly browned.
